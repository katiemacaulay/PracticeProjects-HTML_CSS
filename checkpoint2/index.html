Welcome!

Thank you for coming to my site to learn more about music education at St. Theresa’s Catholic School. We believe that making music is an integral part of serving, student development, and expressing our faith. PreK-6th Grades have music as class integrated into their school year, and Middle School has two new performance based electives: Musical Theater performing The Wizard of Oz in the Spring and Rhythm Cats, our percussion ensemble.

Students at St. Theresa’s not only learn the fundamentals of reading, writing, and performing music, but learn music as a creative and expressive art. Whether students are 5 years old or 13 years old, all students are learning music with the goal of experiencing a wide variety of ways to express ourselves and how to make our own music through composition and improvisation.

Making music is taught in a fun and interactive way, so everytime we have class there is something to look forward to learning.

Musical Theatre

We will focus on vocal technique, collaboration, and performance. Students will have several opportunities to perform; including the Diocesan Choral Festival, Grandparents’ Day, and potentially at a Basketball Game. During Fall Semester students will audition for the Spring Musical. In the Spring semester, students will focus on staging, acting and preparing for the Musical.

Rhythm Cats!

Rhythm Cats! is a percussion ensemble that is a performance oriented ensemble where students will learn the basics of rhythmic reading, percussion instrument techniques, sight-reading, performance etiquette, and musical choreography in order to accomplish short and long term musical goals.

PreK-2nd Grade

Early development of music involves learning to match pitch, basic rhythms, non-pitched percussion playing technique and being able to keep a steady pulse. We focus on music as a tool of expression and use movement a lot to describe musical ideas.

3rd, 4th, 5th & 6th Grade

Upper Elementary involves more improvisation and composition especially using more complex rhythm and melody. Students will learn how to anyalize musical form and pitched instrument techniques as well as hand drumming.
